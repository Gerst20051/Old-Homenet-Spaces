Parenting Teens

Parenting teens is often seen as one long battle. At various stages throughout adolescence your teen is likely to challenge you on one or all of the following:

  • The boundaries you set for them
  • The expectations you have of them
  • The values and beliefs you give to them

This is both normal and healthy. And how you deal with these challenges will often determine how difficult the teenage years will be.

But some parents will have an altogether different experience. They will have a teen who doesn't challenge them in any of these ways. The difficulty for these parents is how to help a teen who may be anxious, timid, or severely lacking in confidence.


 

So what needs to change?

Parenting a teen is all about preparing your teen for adulthood. This means teaching them to think for themselves, to be accountable for their actions and to cope with the emotional ups and downs of life.

And the way in which most teens learn is by doing it themselves. Unfortunately , because parents want to protect their teen, they often fall into the trap of deciding what's best for their teen. Now is the time to start letting go and let your teen stand - or fall - on their own two feet.

This doesn't mean abandoning your teen, far from it. Parenting teens takes time and energy. You need to be able to communicate effectively with your teen so that you can understand what's going on for them.

Resist the urge to be 'the expert' and encourage your teen to think for themselves. Give them responsibility for both themselves and their actions and be supportive when they get it wrong.

Above all, be kind to yourself. If you mess up don't beat yourself up. Remember it's important for your teen to realize that you're human too - something that teens often forget!.
