Understanding Teens

Understanding teens can often seem like an impossible task. When adolescence strikes, many parents feel that their once cute kids have suddenly morphed into aliens. If this were true then things would be much easier;

  • we would accept that they have a different language
     
  • we would accept that they think differently about life
     
  • we would accept that they want to explore and experiment in this new world.

And so it is with teens. The way in which they talk, behave and think changes and continues to change, on what can sometimes seem like a minute by minute basis.

This is all due to the changes that are happening within the teen. Although parents are often aware of the physical and hormonal changes that adolescence brings, not all parents are aware that major changes also occur in the brain.

Understanding the impact of these changes will allow you to help your teen manage them effectively .


 

So what needs to change?

Adolescence is all about experimentation. Teens need to experiment to find out how their feelings, minds and bodies operate in different situations. Providing opportunities for your teen to carry out this experimentation is crucial if they are to develop into happy, well-adjusted adults.

Let them try out their decision-making skills and take responsibility for the results. And yes, sometimes your teen will make poor judgments or not so good decisions. But that is an essential part of the learning process. We often learn far more from our mistakes than we do from our successes.

Supporting your teen through this experimentation will require understanding from both parent and teen. Learning new ways of communicating with your teen will not only reduce your own fears and concerns, but also encourage your teen to act in a mature and adult way.

Understanding your teen is an on-going process. Just as you think you’ve got there, they change again. But then that is part of the pleasure of parenting teens!
