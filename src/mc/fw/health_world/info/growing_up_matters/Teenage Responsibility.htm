Teenage Responsibility

Giving responsibility to your teen is essential if they are to take charge of their adult lives. It is never an easy decision to make and it is normal to think a teenager too young to do this or not ready to do that. But consider the following questions:

  • How soon will your teen be 'leaving home'?
  • What will your teen need to know by then?

Even if your teenager is not physically leaving home, they will have finished school and will be entering a new environment. Whether that environment is further education or work, they will need to be prepared for that change and that preparation needs to start now.

Teens need to be given small doses of responsibility as soon as possible. Most will ask for it, but others will run a mile before accepting any form of responsibility. In either case, the same rules apply.
 

So what needs to change?

Teens need to understand the consequences of their actions and realize the implications on others. This means that when things go wrong, as they undoubtedly will at some stage, they need to actually experience these consequences.

Parents often come in and rescue their teen when things go wrong and this is perfectly natural. No parent wants to see their teen suffer. Unfortunately this is often the worst thing you can do. If your teen doesn’t actually experience the emotional as well as physical consequence, the memory will quickly fade.

Much better that parents support their teens through these situations. Ask the teen how they are going to deal with the consequences and what help do they need. In this way the teen retains ownership of the problem and the responsibility for solving it.

Obviously we are not talking about life and death situations here. But there will be times when you question whether your teen is ready for the responsibility. In fact every time your teen does something for the first time where they are out of your sight, you are likely to feel this. So have a back up plan in place (a topped up mobile phone helps here) and then it is a case of believing in your teen.

And the more you understand your teen and the better communication you have with them, the easier this will be.
