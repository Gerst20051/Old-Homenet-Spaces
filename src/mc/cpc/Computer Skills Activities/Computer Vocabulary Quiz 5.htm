Computer Vocabulary Quiz 5

An online self-grading quiz to help students prepare for the state-wide North Carolina Computer Skills Test given in the fall to 8th Graders. Written by parent Lee Ryan, former Blowing Rock School Webmaster and retired software engineer. September 2001.

Directions: select the best computer vocabulary term for each computer definition or description below. Check your work by clicking on the "Well...How did I do?" button at the end of the quiz.

1.  The physical placement of texts and graphics in a document.

2.  A computerized layout program that integrates graphics and text to produce a professional looking document.

3.  Material protected by copyright laws.

4.  Acknowledgements about the resources you used in a report, document, etc.

5.  A computer program that can reproduce by changing other programs to include a copy of itself. It is a parasite program, needing another program to survive.

6.  Not following a linear path; allows you to make decisions about what information to view next with the use of  icons or hypertext.

7.  A lifelike world that is created by a computer in which participants can become part of the action. A computer-generated, three dimensional imaginary world.

8.  A system of computers linked together to share data, software and hardware.

9.  A collection of printed data organized for search and retrieval. Examples: telephone book, encyclopedia, address book.

10.  The fastest-growing and most user-friendly section of the Internet that lets you access text, graphics, sound and even video.

11.  The practice of not sharing how to access information stored on a computer or computer network. The use of passwords or security services such as encryption to keep unauthorized individuals from gaining access to information stored on a computer or computer network.

12.  Devices that manipulate data in accordance with the instructions of the computer or a program.

13.  A computer document or file that has been previously created, usually for a specific purpose. These documents can be used to find, edit or manipulate information.

14.  Method of animation that transforms one image into another. Morphing was introduced to most of the public in 1993 Michael Jackson music video, and has since become a mainstay of political ads, television commercials, and special effects for movies.

15.  Also called stand alone computers. Computers which are not linked to other computers.  To utilize resources such as printers, modems, scanners, etc., each computer requires its own devices.

16.  Hypertext Markup Language. The primary computer programming language used to make web pages.

17.  "Active text or graphic" where a word, phrase, sentence or graphic is linked to another place in a document, another document, another place in a web page or another web page.  A user-friendly way to connect related textual information. Uses special "links" you can click on to jump from one place to another.

18.  A software program function that checks for possible grammar mistakes. Suggestions or corrections are often given.

19.  1. Computer users who enjoy tinkering with computers. 2. Computer users that illegally break into computer systems to do damage and steal information.

20.  Ideas put into materials, such as writing, music, art, computer code and inventions that can be protected under copyright or patent laws.

21.  A disk-based thesaurus for on screen use.

22.  A collection of data organized for search and retrieval and accessed by computer. Example: CD-ROM Encyclopedias.

23.  Transferring information electronically from another computer to your computer.

24.  Disk-based information for on screen use.

25.  1. The act of tinkering with computers. 2. The act of  illegally breaking into other computer systems to do damage, and steal information
