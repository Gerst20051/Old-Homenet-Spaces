Style and Register:
archaic - not used since pre-WWII
dated - no longer current, but still used by older speakers or authors.
fml - formal: suitable in formal situations and formal writing
humorous - pompous or inflated language used for comic effect
infml - informal: used in relaxed conversation or writing
insult - used for deliberately insulting terms
literary - used in literature, poetry, and quality journalism
nonstandard - not considered part of correct or educated usage
offensive - likely to cause offence, intentionally or unintentionally
regional - used in the dialect of a particular variety of English
slang - used only in very relaxed conversation or writing
technical - used for specialist terms with an everyday equivalent
