BitTorrent Inc. buys µTorrent

ARSTechnica
BitTorrent Inc. announced the acquisition of µTorrent AB, the developer of the popular Windows BitTorrent client of the same name. The terms of the deal have not been disclosed, but it will see the well-liked and lightweight Windows client brought under the BitTorrent umbrella.

In its announcement of the deal, BitTorrent cofounder and CEO Bram Cohen cited µTorrent's "exceptionally well-written codebase" and devoted cadre of users. "Ludvig Strigeus, the developer of uTorrent, clearly put a significant amount of time into optimizing the client, which has already been widely adopted by the BitTorrent community as a safe and efficient implementation of the BitTorrent protocol," said Cohen in a prepared statement.

Heavily used for file-sharing—especially for popular television shows—BitTorrent has recently been making inroads with content creators. In May, BitTorrent and Warner Bros. lifted the curtain on a partnership that would see BitTorrent distributing Warner Bros. TV shows and movies over using its peer-to-peer file-sharing system.

Late last month, BitTorrent added Paramount, Fox, and some cable channels to its TV and movie distribution service, which is currently available only to users of Windows. Users of BitTorrent's commercial service are even able to burn some shows to DVD, although DVD playback is limited to the device on which the content was originally recorded.

BitTorrent plans to integrate its paid content-delivery platform into future versions of µTorrent. By acquiring the application, BitTorrent instantly gains a sizable potential audience for its commercial service. That's the easy part. Convincing µTorrent users to give up the more... conventional uses of their favorite client in favor of the paid content will be a daunting task.
