Communicating with Teens

Many parents would quote communicating with their teen as one of the hardest aspects of parenting a teen. And yet it is one of the most vital if you want to make a difference in the life of your teen. However for many parents the reality of communication means:

  • Telling your teen what to do
  • Telling your teen what's wrong with them
  • Telling your teen to change

The problem is that teens often choose either to ignore this type of communication or to react against it by acting out and being rude. All of which can cause you great distress.

But of course it doesn’t have to be like that. It is possible to have a conversation with your teen that doesn’t consist of grunts, shouting matches or slammed doors.

 

So what needs to change?

For teens to choose to communicate, they have to feel that they have a role in the communication. They need to know they will be listened to and that their feelings and opinions will be taken into account.

So focus on listening rather than talking. Only when you understand what’s going on in the life of your teen can you respond in ways that your teen will find helpful, encouraging and supportive.

Provide opportunities for your teen to show their resourcefulness by not always giving them advice, learn to listen instead. There are many situations where it doesn't matter if your teen get's it 'wrong', so let them try out their own ideas. You might be surprised!

And of course, don't forget that the way in which you talk to your teen is also important. Showing respect and appreciation to your teen is a great way for them to experience and learn how to give respect and appreciation to others.
