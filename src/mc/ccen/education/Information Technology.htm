Information Technology

Course Description:

Students gain a broad understanding of computer operations, networking, and the use of software applications. Students learn how to work in windows environments, Software packages include Microsoft Office (word processing, spreadsheets, databases, and presentations) with the opportunity to test and earn various MOS (Microsoft Office Specialist) certifications. Students receive hands-on experience with state of the art equipment such as microcomputers and interactive computing systems. In addition, skills are gained in networking, accounting, and computer information processing. Sophomore IT students learn Java programming language in preparation for the Advanced Placement Computer Science examination. Junior and Senior IT students prepare for the Cisco Certifies Network Associate (CCNA) examination.
